Case Study 01
Turning a Frustrated Customer into a Retained One
🔧 Tool Used: HubSpot CRM
✓ Resolved
The Problem
A customer complained about a delayed response and an unresolved service request. They were frustrated and actively considering cancelling. Messages had been missed due to no proper tracking system.
My Task
Manage the complaint professionally, ensure nothing fell through the cracks, and resolve the issue using HubSpot CRM — all while maintaining a positive customer experience throughout.
The Stakes
If the issue went unresolved, the business risked losing a paying customer and damaging its reputation. Every missed follow-up was a signal that the client didn't matter.
How I Resolved It — Step by Step
1
Logged the customer issue into HubSpot as a formal support ticket so nothing could get lost or overlooked
2
Reviewed the full interaction history to understand the root cause before responding — not just the surface complaint
3
Updated the ticket status and flagged it as high priority for immediate attention
4
Responded to the customer with a professional, empathetic message that acknowledged the delay without deflecting blame
5
Set automated reminders and follow-up tasks within HubSpot to ensure the issue was tracked to full resolution
6
Coordinated the resolution and proactively updated the customer at each stage — no silence, no guessing
📈 Outcome
The issue was fully resolved within a short timeframe. The customer responded positively to the timely, transparent communication and chose to stay. This demonstrated that a well-managed complaint — when handled with empathy and structure — can actually strengthen a client relationship.
✓ Customer retained
✓ Issue resolved with CRM tracking
✓ Zero missed follow-ups
Case Study 02
Building a Customer Support System from Scratch
🔧 Tools Used: HubSpot CRM + Ticket Pipeline
✓ Implemented
The Problem
A company was experiencing delayed responses to customer inquiries. Messages were being missed, customers were sending repeated follow-ups, and satisfaction was dropping — all because there was no proper tracking system.
My Task
Design and implement an organized customer support system using HubSpot that would centralize all inquiries, improve response consistency, and make it impossible for tickets to be ignored.
The Stakes
Without a system, the team would keep operating reactively — always behind, always firefighting. The business needed a structure that worked even on busy days.
What I Built — Step by Step
1
Centralized all customer inquiries by logging them into HubSpot for ticket tracking and organized follow-up management
2
Created a structured pipeline with clear stages: New → In Progress → Awaiting Response → Resolved — so every ticket had a visible status
3
Prioritized urgent issues by assigning ticket importance levels so critical problems rose to the top automatically
4
Set up HubSpot task reminders to follow up on unresolved tickets — removing the reliance on human memory
5
Monitored the pipeline regularly to ensure no ticket stagnated and response times stayed consistent
📈 Outcome
The new system improved response consistency and eliminated missed customer inquiries. Customers received faster, more organized updates. The team went from reactive to proactive — and the difference was visible immediately in how communication flowed.
✓ Zero missed inquiries
✓ Faster response times
✓ Full pipeline visibility
✓ Team went from reactive → proactive
Case Study 03
Organizing Chaos: A Trello Workflow for Customer Support
🔧 Tool Used: Trello
✓ Delivered
The Problem
A small business was struggling to manage customer requests efficiently. Messages were disorganized, follow-ups were missed, and there was no clear system to track progress from first contact to final resolution.
My Task
Design and implement a simple, visual workflow using Trello that would organize customer support activities, improve task visibility, ensure timely responses, and track every issue from start to resolution.
My Approach
I combined project management principles with customer support logic — building a board that any team member could pick up and use immediately, without needing a manual.
How I Built It
1
Audited the existing communication flow to identify exactly where issues were falling through the gaps
2
Designed a Trello board with columns reflecting the customer journey: New Request → In Progress → Awaiting Customer → Resolved
3
Created card templates with due dates, labels, and checklists so every task had clear ownership and a deadline
4
Coordinated the planning and implementation — structured workflows, tracked progress, and ensured all tasks were completed within the project timeline
📈 Outcome
The business now had a more organized and efficient customer support system. Inquiries were tracked clearly, response consistency improved, and the chances of a customer message going unanswered dropped to near zero. This project demonstrated my ability to combine project management with customer support to deliver real, usable systems.
